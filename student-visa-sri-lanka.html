Core Documents

Common Visa Documents for All Countries

Regardless of destination, most student visas from Sri Lanka require these core documents. Navixa prepares and reviews every single one.

🛂

Valid Passport

Passport valid for at least 6 months beyond your intended stay. Min 2 blank pages. Apply for renewal well in advance if expiring soon.

📜

University Admission Letter

Unconditional offer letter from your accepted university, showing course name, duration, start date, and tuition fee details.

💰

Financial Proof

Bank statements, fixed deposits, or blocked account confirmation showing sufficient funds. Requirements vary by country (€8,000–£30,000+).

📋

Academic Transcripts

Certified copies of O/L, A/L results (for bachelor's) or degree transcripts and certificates (for master's). Often need apostille or notarization.

🏥

Health Insurance

Comprehensive travel/health insurance for the full duration of study. Must meet destination country requirements for coverage amount.

📝

Motivation Letter / SOP

Personal statement explaining why you chose the country, university, and program. A well-written SOP significantly strengthens your application.

Top Tips

Visa Success Tips for Sri Lankan Students

💡

Start Early — Very Early

German visa takes 4–6 months total (APS + application + processing). UK takes 2–3 months. Italy 4–5 months. Always start the process at least 6 months before your intended departure.

💡

Perfect Your Financial Documents

Embassy officers scrutinize financial documents most carefully. All bank statements must show consistent balance, no sudden deposits. Navixa reviews your financial documents before submission.

💡

Write a Strong Motivation Letter

Your SOP/motivation letter can make or break your application. Be specific about why this university, this program, and this country. Navixa has written 150+ approved SOPs.

💡

Prepare for Embassy Interview

Germany and some other countries conduct short interviews. Common questions: Why this university? Why Germany? What will you do after graduation? Navixa conducts mock interviews to ensure you're prepared.

💡

Don't Apply Alone

Visa rejections due to documentation errors can result in 6-month or longer bans. With 98% visa success rate, Navixa ensures your application is perfect before submission.

FAQs

Student Visa FAQs

Which European country gives the easiest student visa from Sri Lanka? +
The UK generally has the fastest and most straightforward process (3–4 weeks processing) but requires financial evidence in GBP. Germany has more steps (APS certificate required) but is slower (6–12 weeks). Italy has a unique Declaration of Value requirement. Malta is generally the simplest for entry-level. Navixa recommends the best route based on your profile and timeline.
Can my parents' bank account be used for financial proof? +
Yes! For most countries, you can show financial sponsorship from parents. You'll need: parents' bank statements (usually 6 months), a sponsor letter, and proof of parents' income (salary slips, business documents). The funds must be maintained consistently, not deposited suddenly before applying.
What happens if my student visa is rejected? +
A visa rejection doesn't mean permanent rejection. Common reasons: insufficient financial evidence, incomplete documents, weak motivation letter, or unconvincing study plans. With Navixa's guidance, rejection rates are extremely low. If rejected, we identify the reason and help you reapply with a stronger application.
Can I bring my family (spouse/children) on a dependent visa? +
Yes, most European countries allow dependents (spouse and minor children) to join you. Requirements vary — generally, you need to show sufficient additional funds to support dependents and proof of relationship (marriage certificate, birth certificates). Navixa handles dependent visa applications alongside your student visa.
