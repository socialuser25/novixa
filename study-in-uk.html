Visa Process

UK Student Visa Process

1

Apply to Universities

Apply via UCAS (undergraduate) or direct to universities (postgraduate). Navixa helps shortlist and apply to 5–6 universities matching your profile. Aim for 6–12 months before your start date.

2

Get Your CAS Number

After unconditional admission, university issues a Confirmation of Acceptance for Studies (CAS). This 14-digit number is mandatory for your visa application and contains your course and fee details.

3

Apply for Student Visa

Apply online at gov.uk. Required documents: passport, CAS number, 28-day bank statement showing required funds, tuberculosis test results, IELTS/TOEFL certificate, academic transcripts.

4

Pay Fees & Book Biometrics

Pay £490 visa fee + NHS Immigration Health Surcharge (£776/year). Book biometrics appointment at UK Visa Application Centre in Colombo. Give fingerprints and photo.

5

Receive Visa & Travel

Standard processing 3–4 weeks. Priority 5 working days available. Visa allows entry 1 month before course start. Collect your Biometric Residence Permit on arrival.

FAQs

UK Study FAQs

What IELTS score do I need for UK universities? +
Most UK universities require IELTS 6.0–6.5 (undergraduate) and 6.5–7.0 (postgraduate). Russell Group universities typically require 7.0+. Some accept TOEFL or PTE as alternatives.
Can I work while studying in the UK? +
Yes! UK student visa allows 20 hours/week during term time and unlimited hours during holidays. Minimum wage is £11.44/hour. Part-time work significantly helps cover living costs.
What is the Graduate Route Visa? +
The Graduate Route lets you stay in the UK 2 years after your degree (3 years for PhD). You can work in any job. This is your bridge to finding a sponsored employer and getting a long-term work visa.
Are there scholarships for Sri Lankans? +
Yes! Chevening Scholarships (fully funded), Commonwealth Scholarships, British Council grants, and university-specific merit scholarships are all available. Navixa advises on which scholarships match your profile.
