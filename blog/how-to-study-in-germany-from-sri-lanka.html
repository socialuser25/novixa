🇩🇪 Germany Guide

How to Study in Germany from Sri Lanka in 2026

The complete, step-by-step guide. Updated for 2026 requirements.

📅 March 2026 ✍️ Navixa Education Team ⏱️ 8 min read 👁️ 4,312 readers

Germany is the dream destination for thousands of Sri Lankan students every year — and for good reason. Most public universities charge zero tuition fees (even for international students), the economy is booming with job opportunities, and an 18-month job seeker visa after graduation makes it the ultimate launchpad for a European career.

In this complete guide, we'll walk you through every single step — from checking your eligibility all the way to your first day at a German university.

💡 Quick Summary: German universities require an APS certificate, language proof (German or English), and a blocked account for the visa. Processing takes 4–8 months total. Start planning at least 12 months before your desired enrollment date.

Step 1: Check Your Eligibility

Before applying to any German university, you need to confirm you meet the basic academic requirements. Different programs have different thresholds, but here are the general minimums for Sri Lankan students:

  • For Bachelor's Programs: Sri Lanka A/L with at least 3 subjects. You must also have the APS certificate (explained below). Some universities may require specific subject combinations depending on your field.
  • For Master's Programs: A completed Bachelor's degree (minimum 3 years, 180 ECTS or equivalent). Your GPA matters — most competitive programs expect a minimum GPA of 3.0/4.0 or equivalent.
  • For PhD Programs: Master's degree from a recognized university. Research proposal required.

Step 2: Get Your APS Certificate

This is the most unique requirement for Sri Lankan students. The Academic Evaluation Centre (APS) is a verification body that confirms the authenticity of your Sri Lankan educational documents for German universities.

Without the APS certificate, your application will be rejected by virtually all German universities. Here's how to get it:

  • Apply at the German Embassy in Colombo (not online — you must go in person)
  • Required documents: Original O/L and A/L certificates, official translations, passport, application form
  • Processing time: approximately 6–8 weeks
  • Cost: approximately LKR 25,000
  • The APS certificate is issued by email and is valid indefinitely

⚠️ Important: The APS backlog in Sri Lanka can be long. Apply for your APS certificate as early as possible — ideally 3–4 months before your university application deadlines.

Step 3: Choose Your Language Path

Germany has two types of programs for international students:

  • German-taught programs: Require TestDaF Level 4 or DSH-2 or Goethe C1 certificate. More options available (thousands of programs). Takes 12–18 months of intensive German study to reach required level from scratch.
  • English-taught programs: Require IELTS 6.0–6.5 or TOEFL 80–90+. Growing number of programs, especially at Master's level. Many top engineering and business programs available in English.

Navixa's recommendation: If your goal is to stay and work in Germany long-term, learning German opens far more doors. If you want faster admission and flexibility, English-taught programs are excellent.

Step 4: Select and Apply to Universities

Germany has over 400 universities, ranging from world-famous research institutions (TU Munich, Heidelberg) to excellent applied science universities (Fachhochschulen) with strong industry connections.

Apply through:

  • uni-assist.de: Centralized portal accepting applications for 170+ German universities. Apply 6–12 months before your start date.
  • Direct university portals: Some universities (like TU Munich) have their own application systems. Always check individual university websites.
  • DAAD scholarship portal: If applying for German academic scholarships (DAAD), apply separately at daad.de.

Step 5: Set Up Your Blocked Account

After receiving your university admission letter, you must set up a German blocked account (Sperrkonto) to apply for your visa. The blocked account holds your proof of financial resources.

  • Required amount: €11,208 per year (2025 figure, updated annually)
  • This money is released to you monthly (€934/month) while you're in Germany
  • Best providers: Fintiba (recommended), Expatrio, Deutsche Bank
  • Opening takes 1–2 weeks; Navixa guides you through the process
  • You'll receive a confirmation letter to include with your visa application

Step 6: Apply for Your Germany Student Visa

Now for the visa itself. Gather these documents:

  • Valid passport (6+ months validity beyond your stay)
  • University admission letter
  • APS certificate
  • Blocked account confirmation
  • Language test certificate (TestDaF/IELTS)
  • Health insurance confirmation (TK, AOK, or Barmer)
  • Enrollment documents and course details
  • Motivation letter for visa
  • 2 biometric passport photos
  • Visa application form (filled online at videx.diplo.de)

Book your appointment at the German Embassy Colombo (Jawatta Road). Appointment waiting times are 4–8 weeks. Processing after submission: 6–12 weeks typically.

Step 7: Arrive in Germany

Congratulations — you have your visa! Here's what to do within your first 2 weeks:

  • Register your address (Anmeldung) at the local Bürgeramt — mandatory within 14 days
  • Enroll at your university and get your student ID card
  • Activate your German health insurance (TK, AOK)
  • Open a German bank account (N26, DKB, or Sparkasse)
  • Get a German SIM card (O2, Telekom, Vodafone)
  • Apply to university accommodation or confirm private housing
  • Get your public transport semester ticket (usually included in semester fee!)

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does it cost to study in Germany for Sri Lankan students? +
Main upfront cost: €11,208 blocked account. Monthly living costs: €700–1,200 (rent + food + transport + personal). Tuition at public universities: €0–350/semester. Part-time work (120 days/year) earns €800–1,500/month to offset costs.
Can I study in Germany without knowing German? +
Yes — choose an English-taught program (IELTS 6.0+ required). However, for daily life in Germany, basic German is very helpful. We recommend starting German classes even if you choose an English program.
What is the APS and how long does it take? +
APS (Academic Evaluation Centre) verifies your Sri Lankan educational documents for German universities. Apply at the German Embassy Colombo. Takes 6–8 weeks and costs ~LKR 25,000. Required for all Sri Lankan applicants.
