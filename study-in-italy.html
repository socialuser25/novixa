Why Italy?

Why Study in Italy

Italy combines rich academic tradition (Bologna is the world's oldest university) with generous scholarships, affordable costs, and Schengen travel access.

💰

DSU Scholarships

Italian regional scholarship grants (DSU) provide up to €6,000+ per year covering tuition fee waiver, accommodation, and monthly stipend. Available to Sri Lankan students!

🏛️

Ancient & Prestigious Universities

University of Bologna (est. 1088), Sapienza Rome, Politecnico di Milano — ancient institutions with modern programs, all globally ranked.

💸

Low Cost of Living

Outside major cities like Milan and Rome, Italy is very affordable. Student cities like Bologna, Turin, Naples offer excellent value for money.

🌍

Schengen Travel

Italian student visa gives Schengen access — travel to 26+ European countries on weekends and holidays. Explore Europe while studying!

🇬🇧

English Programs Available

Politecnico di Milano and many other Italian universities offer full English-medium Bachelor's and Master's programs — no Italian required!

🎨

Art, Culture & Lifestyle

Italy's lifestyle, food, art, and culture are unmatched. Study in Florence, Rome or Venice — some of the world's most beautiful cities.

Application Process

Italy Study Visa Step by Step

1

Apply to Italian University

Most Italian universities open applications January–April for September intake. Apply directly to the university website or through Universitaly.it portal. Navixa helps identify the best universities for your profile and DSU eligibility.

2

Get Declaration of Value

Required document: Declaration of Value (Dichiarazione di Valore) of your Sri Lankan certificates, obtained from the Italian Embassy in Colombo. Navixa guides you through this process. Takes 4–6 weeks.

3

Receive University Acceptance

Once admitted, collect your enrolment confirmation, DSU scholarship application (if eligible), and all supporting documents needed for the visa application.

4

Apply for Italy Student Visa

Apply at the Italian Embassy Colombo for a Type D study visa. Required: admission letter, passport, financial proof, health insurance, accommodation proof, declaration of value. Navixa prepares all documents.

5

Arrive & Get Residence Permit

On arrival, apply for a Permesso di Soggiorno (residence permit) within 8 days at the local police station. Enroll in the municipality's residence registry. Collect student ID and start your Italian adventure!

FAQs

Italy Study FAQs

Do I need to know Italian to study in Italy? +
Not necessarily! Many Italian universities offer full English-medium programs, especially at Politecnico di Milano, Bocconi, and LUISS. For Italian-taught programs, you need a B2 certificate. Learning even basic Italian greatly improves your daily life experience.
How much does a Sri Lankan student need to live in Italy? +
Monthly costs: rent €300–700 (student cities cheaper), food €200–300, transport €30–60, other €100-200. Total: €700–1,300/month. With DSU scholarship covering accommodation and providing stipend, actual out-of-pocket costs can be very low.
What is the DSU scholarship and who qualifies? +
DSU is Italy's need-based scholarship system for university students. International students qualify based on family income (ISEE-E calculation). Most Sri Lankan families qualify. Benefits include tuition waiver, cash grant, free or subsidized accommodation and meals.
Can I work while studying in Italy? +
Yes! Students can work up to 20 hours per week in Italy. Minimum wage varies by sector (€8–12/hour typically). Italy has a growing tech and tourism sector with opportunities for students.
