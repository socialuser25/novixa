Why Germany?

Why Sri Lankan Students Choose Germany

Germany offers one of the world's best education systems, with most public universities charging little to no tuition fees — even for international students from Sri Lanka.

💰

No/Low Tuition Fees

Most German public universities charge only a semester fee of €100–€350. No tuition fees means you save thousands annually.

🏆

World-Ranked Universities

TU Munich, LMU Munich, Heidelberg, and 80+ universities in global top rankings. Degrees respected worldwide.

💼

18-Month Job Seeker Visa

After graduation, you get 18 months to find a job. With Germany's booming economy, most Sri Lankans get hired quickly.

🌍

Gateway to Europe

Study in Germany = access to all 27 EU countries. Travel, work, and build your career across Europe.

👨‍💻

Part-Time Work Rights

International students can work 120 full days or 240 half days per year while studying in Germany.

🇱🇰

Growing Sri Lankan Community

Thousands of Sri Lankans already live and thrive in Germany. Strong support networks in Berlin, Munich, and Frankfurt.

Visa Process

Step-by-Step Germany Student Visa Process

From application to arrival — here's your complete roadmap for Sri Lankan students.

1

Get Your APS Certificate

Sri Lankan students MUST obtain an APS (Academic Evaluation Centre) certificate. This verifies your Sri Lankan O/L and A/L results. Apply at the German Embassy Colombo. Processing takes 6–8 weeks. Cost: ~LKR 25,000.

2

Pass Language Requirement

For German-taught programs: TestDaF Level 4 or DSH-2. For English-taught programs: IELTS 6.0+ or TOEFL 80+. Many universities also accept GRE for Master's programs. Start language prep early — minimum 6 months.

3

Apply to Universities

Apply via uni-assist (centralized German admissions portal) or directly to the university admissions office. Apply 6–12 months before your intended start date. German universities typically start in October (Winter Semester) or April (Summer Semester).

4

Set Up Blocked Account

Once admitted, open a German blocked account (Sperrkonto) with providers like Fintiba or Expatrio. Transfer €11,208 (for 2025). This proves financial sufficiency to the German embassy. Monthly amount released to you while studying.

5

Prepare Visa Documents

Required: Valid passport, admission letter, APS certificate, blocked account confirmation, health insurance, CV, motivation letter, financial proof, passport photos. Navixa prepares and reviews all your documents.

6

Book Embassy Appointment

Book your appointment at the German Embassy in Colombo. Wait times can be 4–8 weeks so book early! Attend the interview (usually short with proper preparation). Visa is usually processed within 6–12 weeks after interview.

7

Arrive & Register in Germany

Register your address (Anmeldung) within 14 days of arrival. Enroll at your university. Open a German bank account. Register for health insurance. Get your student ID and public transport pass. You're officially a German student! 🎉

FAQs

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I study in Germany without IELTS? +
Yes! If you studied in an English-medium school or university, many German universities accept a medium of instruction certificate instead of IELTS. Additionally, if you choose a German-language program, you need TestDaF or DSH instead of IELTS. Ask us which universities accept your background.
What is an APS certificate and how do I get it? +
APS (Academic Evaluation Centre) is a mandatory document verification for Sri Lankan students applying to German universities. Apply at the German Embassy Colombo with your original O/L and A/L certificates. Cost is approximately LKR 25,000 and takes 6–8 weeks. Navixa guides you through the entire process.
How much does it cost for Sri Lankans to study in Germany? +
The main costs are: Blocked account €11,208 (held as security, returned monthly), tuition €0–350/semester at public universities, monthly living costs €700–€1,200 (rent €350–600 + food + transport). Part-time work helps cover daily expenses. Compared to UK or USA, Germany is dramatically more affordable.
Can I work while studying in Germany? +
Yes! International students in Germany can work up to 120 full days (or 240 half days) per year. Students typically earn €12–€18/hour. Working 20 hours/week can cover most of your living costs. Universities also offer student assistant (HiWi) positions.
What are the best universities in Germany for Sri Lankan students? +
Top choices include: TU Munich, LMU Munich, Heidelberg University, TU Berlin, RWTH Aachen, University of Stuttgart, Karlsruhe Institute of Technology (KIT), and Hamburg University. Best fields: Engineering, Computer Science, Business, Life Sciences. We help you choose based on your profile and goals.
How long does the Germany student visa take? +
The Germany student visa processing time from Sri Lanka is typically 6–12 weeks after submitting complete documents. Embassy appointment waiting time is 4–8 weeks. We recommend starting the process at least 6 months before your intended departure date. Navixa helps ensure all documents are perfect to avoid delays.
